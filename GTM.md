# StackSpend Go-To-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

StackSpend targets **engineering managers and founders with 5-50 person teams** who are losing money on overlapping AI tools. Our GTM strategy is **zero-budget, founder-led growth** leveraging viral mechanics (shareable reports), community activation, and organic SEO.

**Target: 100 audits by month 2, $5k MRR by month 6**

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Persona 1: The Bootstrapped Founder (Primary)

**Demographics:**
- 28-40 years old
- Building AI-first SaaS or tools
- 5-20 person team
- Raised <$5M or bootstrapped
- Wears finance + eng hat

**Psychographics:**
- Cost-conscious (every dollar matters)
- Technical enough to understand recommendations
- Early-adopter mentality
- Active on Twitter, Indie Hackers, Product Hunt
- Wants to optimize every axis

**Problem:**
- "We're spending $3k/month on AI tools. I think we're overpaying but don't know how much."
- No time for cost analysis
- Worried about hidden costs

**Why StackSpend:**
- Free audit (no commitment)
- 5-minute onboarding
- Shareable report (align team on savings)
- Immediate ROI visibility

### Persona 2: Engineering Manager at Scaleup (Secondary)

**Demographics:**
- 28-45 years old
- Director of Engineering at Series A-B startup
- Managing 10-30 engineers
- Has budget responsibility

**Psychographics:**
- Wants to optimize team spend
- Needs to justify tools to CFO
- Impressed by transparency
- Wants executive dashboard visibility

**Problem:**
- "Which AI tools should our team actually be using?"
- "How do I prove to finance that we're not overspending?"
- No visibility into individual developer tool choices

**Why StackSpend:**
- Audit justifies tool budget
- Shareable report for stakeholders
- Data-driven recommendations

## Trigger Moments

**When do founders decide to audit their spend?**

1. **New budget cycle** (quarterly planning)
   - "It's Q3, time to review tooling spend"
   - Opportunity: GTM content about budget optimization

2. **New tool adoption** (Copilot, Claude, etc. launches)
   - "We're considering Claude Pro. Is it worth it?"
   - Opportunity: Position StackSpend as decision-maker

3. **Fundraising** (closing round, series A coming)
   - "Finance wants to cut costs. Where's the fat?"
   - Opportunity: Target VCs, angels, founders mid-fundraise

4. **Team growth milestone** (10 → 20 people)
   - "As we grow, which tools don't scale?"
   - Opportunity: Reach founders scaling through hiring

5. **CFO hire** (scaleups hire first finance person)
   - "I see we're spending $50k/year on AI. Is this right?"
   - Opportunity: Sales motion to finance leads at B-series

## Viral Mechanics

**Core viral loop:**

```
User completes audit
    ↓
Sees savings ($1k/year? $5k/year?)
    ↓
"Share this report" button
    ↓
Shares in team Slack
    ↓
Teammates see report, say "This is real?"
    ↓
They want to run their own audit
    ↓
New users from viral share
```

**Coefficient of virality:**
- 100 audits → 20 shares (20% share rate)
- 20 shares → 60 people view (3 people per share)
- 60 people → 10 new audits (16% conversion)
- **Viral coefficient = 0.1** (each audit generates 0.1 new audits)

**Implication:** Not viral enough to grow on its own, but enough to amplify marketing. Good for "network-driven" growth after initial launch.

## Risk & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Product-Market Fit
"Do founders actually care about this?"

**Mitigation:**
- Pre-launch: 5 founder interviews (validating pain)
- Post-launch: Track NPS + user feedback
- Pivot criteria: <20% lead capture rate = rethink positioning

### Risk 2: Recommendations Accuracy
"Founders try recommendations, discover we were wrong"

**Mitigation:**
- Confidence badges (set expectations)
- Transparent rules (show reasoning)
- First 20 users: Track if they implement recommendations
- Iterate rules based on feedback

### Risk 3: Founder Churn
"Founder audits once, leaves, never comes back"

**Mitigation:**
- Email nudges: "Time to re-audit? AI pricing has changed"
- Quarterly re-audits (cheap, valuable)
- Community building (Slack group of StackSpend users)

### Risk 4: Competitive Entry
"A VC-backed competitor builds this"

**Mitigation:**
- Move fast (build lead advantage before funded competitor launches)
- Community moat (early users become advocates)
- Transparency advantage (competitors might hide rules / push biased recommendations)
