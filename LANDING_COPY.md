## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "AI pricing varies. Your numbers might be wrong."

**Response (In FAQ):**
"Our recommendations are based on public vendor pricing. We show confidence levels so you know where uncertainty exists. Always validate against your actual usage before implementing. We recommend confirming big changes with your vendor."

### Objection 2: "We need Claude for capabilities ChatGPT doesn't have."

**Response:**
"That's valid. Our recommendations assume fungible usage. If you have specific use cases that require a tool, keep it. Our recommendations flag where you might consolidate; final decisions are yours."

### Objection 3: "Switching tools is disruptive to our team."

**Response:**
"Switching costs are real. That's why we show savings in clear dollar amounts. If the monthly savings is higher than your switching cost, it's usually worth it. We show payback time for that reason."

### Objection 4: "We already optimize our spending."

**Response (Badge in report):**
"Great! If StackSpend flags your setup as already optimized, you'll see a badge confirming it. That's valuable too—you know you're making good decisions."

## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Tone: Honest, Helpful, Not Hyped

✓ DO:
- Show confidence levels (not false certainty)
- Explain reasoning (show your work)
- Acknowledge limitations (we could be wrong)
- Give users agency (you decide, not us)

✗ DON'T:
- Make guarantees (savings aren't guaranteed)
- Hide caveats (pricing might differ)
- Use hype ("AI magic," "game-changer")
- Oversell (be realistic about what we do)

### Examples of Good Copy
- "You could save $1,200/year. Depends on implementation." ✓ (Qualified, honest)
- "Our recommendations are 80% accurate based on public pricing." ✓ (Specific, not hype)
- "Confidence: Moderate. Validate before switching." ✓ (Sets expectations)

### Examples of Bad Copy
- "Save up to $50k/year!" ✗ (Vague, misleading)
- "AI-powered recommendations" ✗ (Hype, we use rules not ML)
- "Guaranteed savings" ✗ (Can't guarantee; depends on user)
